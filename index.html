Covid-19 in Sweden

Sweden goes Herd Immunity

Herd immunity (also called herd effect, community immunity, population immunity, or social immunity) is a form of indirect protection from infectious disease that occurs when a sufficient percentage of a population has become immune to an infection, whether through vaccination or previous infections, thereby reducing the likelihood of infection for individuals who lack immunity. cited from Wikipedia

Northern Countries

The highest number of confirmed coronavirus (COVID-19) cases in the Nordic countries as of November 18, 2020, was in Sweden, where the number amounted to 196,446. Denmark followed with 65,808 cases, and Norway with 29,510 cases. cited from Covid‑19 in Nordic

Why & Media Reactions

The Swedish legislation doesn not allow the govenment to lockdown the sociaty, including public traffic, schools, restaurants and entertainment and sport branches. The govenment refers very often to the institutions, and this time it's the Public Health Agency of Sweden's turn to shine. However, this agency chose to go Herd Immunity and insists it is going right. Journalists and experts including professors and virologs critisized the agency's stratage very often. However, the govenment and the agency always find excuse to explain the consequecies.
